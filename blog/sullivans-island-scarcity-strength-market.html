Scarcity, Strength, and a Market Unlike Anywhere Else

Austin Garland, Realtor
The Cassina Group · Mount Pleasant, SC
March 2026

Along the Charleston coastline, few places carry the prestige, charm, and enduring demand of Sullivan's Island. While many real estate markets across the country have experienced fluctuations, Sullivan's Island continues to demonstrate remarkable resilience. Here, scarcity defines the market — and value follows.

Values Continue to Climb

Over the past several years, property values on Sullivan's Island have steadily appreciated. The combination of limited land, historic character, and proximity to Charleston creates a rare coastal offering that continues to attract discerning buyers.

Unlike larger resort destinations, Sullivan's Island maintains a small-town scale with strict development and rental limitations. This naturally restricts supply, which has historically supported long-term property values — even during periods when other markets soften.

Inventory Remains Exceptionally Limited

Inventory on Sullivan's Island is consistently scarce. At any given time, only a handful of homes may be publicly available for sale. Many homeowners hold their properties for generations, further tightening supply.

Because of this scarcity, well-positioned homes can still attract multiple offers when they come to market. Buyers seeking a foothold on the island often wait months — or even years — for the right opportunity to appear.

Waterfront Opportunities Are Rare

Waterfront properties on Sullivan's Island are among the most coveted along the South Carolina coast. Homes with unobstructed ocean views or direct water access rarely reach the open market.

When these properties do become available, they typically generate significant interest from buyers who understand both the lifestyle and long-term value associated with owning property on the island.

A Different Buyer Profile

One of the defining characteristics of the Sullivan's Island market is its buyer profile. Many purchasers are financially well-positioned and motivated by lifestyle rather than market timing. This dynamic often insulates the market from the volatility seen in other parts of the country.

Buyers here are seeking privacy, architectural character, and a connection to the coast — elements that simply cannot be replicated elsewhere.

The Importance of Access

In a market where inventory is limited, access matters.

Many of the most desirable opportunities on Sullivan's Island never reach the public market or the MLS. Through long-standing relationships and a strong local network, I often have insight into properties that are quietly preparing to sell or being offered privately.

For buyers serious about securing a home on Sullivan's Island, early access can make all the difference.

Looking Ahead

Sullivan's Island remains one of the most exclusive and tightly held coastal communities in the Southeast. With limited inventory, strong demand, and a buyer base focused on long-term ownership, the market continues to operate on its own terms.

If you are considering purchasing or selling on Sullivan's Island, having the right connections — and the right strategy — can open doors that others simply never see.

I know the market because I work in it constantly, not because I read a report.
